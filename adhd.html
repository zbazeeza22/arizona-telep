Signs of ADHD in Adults

ADHD looks different in adults than it does in children. Many adults with ADHD were never diagnosed as kids. Here are the most common signs our patients recognize in themselves before seeking an evaluation.

Difficulty focusing

Struggling to sustain attention on tasks, especially ones that feel repetitive or unstimulating, even when you know they matter.

Chronic lateness

Consistently underestimating how long things take, leading to being late for work, appointments, and social commitments.

Unfinished projects

Starting many things but struggling to follow through. A pattern of half-done tasks at work and at home.

Impulsive decisions

Acting without thinking — interrupting others, making snap purchases, or saying things you immediately regret.

Emotional overwhelm

Intense emotional reactions that seem disproportionate. Quick to frustration, easily overwhelmed by stress or criticism.

Losing things constantly

Frequently misplacing keys, phone, wallet, or important documents despite your best efforts to stay organized.

Disorganization

Cluttered workspace, missed deadlines, and difficulty prioritizing tasks even with reminders and to-do lists.

Hyperfocus episodes

Losing hours in something interesting while ignoring everything else. Hyperfocus can feel productive but often comes at the cost of responsibilities.

Restlessness

An internal sense of restlessness or the need to always be doing something. Difficulty relaxing or sitting through long meetings.

Sound familiar? You are not alone.

What Happens During Your ADHD Evaluation

Wondering how to get tested for ADHD in Arizona? Here is exactly what to expect during your evaluation at Arizona Telepsychiatry Clinic.

1

Pre-visit intake forms

After booking, you will complete intake forms online covering your medical history, current medications, and the symptoms that concern you most. This typically takes 10-15 minutes.

2

ASRS screening questionnaire

You will also complete the Adult ADHD Self-Report Scale (ASRS), a validated screening tool used worldwide to assess ADHD symptoms in adults. This is not a diagnosis by itself -- it gives your provider valuable data.

3

Medical and psychiatric history review

Your NP reviews your full medical history, past mental health treatment, family history, and any medications or supplements you currently take. This helps rule out other conditions that can mimic ADHD.

4

Clinical interview

The core of your evaluation. Your provider asks about your daily functioning, work performance, relationships, and how symptoms affect your life. This conversation is where your lived experience matters most.

5

Diagnosis discussion

Your provider explains what the evaluation reveals. If you meet criteria for ADHD, they will explain which type and how it presents in your case. If not, they will discuss alternative explanations.

6

Treatment plan and next steps

Together, you build a treatment plan that may include medication, behavioral strategies, therapy referrals, or a combination. Prescriptions are sent electronically to your pharmacy if appropriate.

ADHD Treatment Options in Arizona

Can you get ADHD medication through telehealth? Yes. Our provider prescribes both stimulant and non-stimulant medications when clinically appropriate, and also supports non-medication approaches. Here are the treatment paths available to you.

Medication management

Stimulant medications (such as Adderall, Vyvanse, or Ritalin) and non-stimulant options (like Strattera or Wellbutrin) are prescribed when clinically appropriate after a thorough evaluation.

  • E-prescriptions sent to your pharmacy
  • Ongoing dosage adjustments
  • Side effect monitoring

Behavioral strategies

Your provider works with you on practical strategies to manage ADHD symptoms day-to-day, including time management techniques, organizational systems, and habit-building approaches.

  • Personalized coping strategies
  • Therapy referrals when appropriate
  • Lifestyle and routine optimization
Most common

Combination approach

Research shows that combining medication with behavioral strategies produces the best outcomes for most adults with ADHD. Your provider helps you build a plan that works for your life.

  • Medication + behavioral support
  • Regular follow-ups to fine-tune
  • Best long-term outcomes

A note on controlled substances

We approach ADHD medication with clinical responsibility and transparency. Stimulant medications are Schedule II controlled substances. They are prescribed only after a thorough evaluation confirms an ADHD diagnosis and when the clinical benefits outweigh risks. We follow all DEA regulations and Arizona Board of Nursing prescribing guidelines. Our goal is safe, effective, evidence-based care.
