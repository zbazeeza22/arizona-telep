First telepsychiatry visit

How to prepare for your first online ADHD appointment in Arizona

Practical checklist for Arizona patients

Before the visit

  • Write down your main goals—for example: “understand if I have ADHD” or “get help focusing at work.”
  • Gather any old report cards, psychological testing, or previous medication lists if you have them.
  • Test your internet connection and video setup in a quiet, private space in Arizona where you won’t be interrupted.

During the telepsychiatry appointment

  • Expect questions about childhood, school, work, and how symptoms show up day‑to‑day.
  • Be honest about what’s hard—even if it feels “minor.” Small details often clarify the diagnosis.
  • Ask about next steps in Arizona: lab work, follow‑up visits, therapy referrals, and medication options if appropriate.

After the visit

  • Add follow‑up visits to your calendar so you stay on track with the plan.
  • Keep a brief symptom or productivity log for the first few weeks so you and your clinician can see patterns.

Whether you live in Phoenix, Tucson, Flagstaff, or anywhere else in the state, telepsychiatry makes it easier to access specialist ADHD care without long drives or time off work.
